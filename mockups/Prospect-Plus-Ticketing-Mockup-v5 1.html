HOME / WORKFLOWS

Scenario workflows

How a ticket moves through the system in each common scenario, mapped to Kaley's specification (statuses §2.1, no-movement exceptions §4.4, investigation actions §4.6, master case §6, FreightSafe). Boxes are statuses/actions, diamonds are decisions, and the ⏸ pause markers show where the SLA clock stops.

Ticket lifecycle Delayed / No-movement Dispute of Delivery Damaged / FreightSafe Check Address (depot) Multi-consignment master case ✉ Email automation

Standard ticket lifecycle

Every ticket follows this workflow stage path. Outcome tags (Lost in Transit, Damaged, FreightSafe Eligible) are a separate field and can be applied at any stage. The SLA clock pauses on Awaiting Operations and Awaiting Customer.
Start In progress Paused (external) Resolved ⏸ = SLA clock stops

Delayed Item / no-movement exception

Triggered by a customer "Delayed Item" enquiry or automatically when a consignment has no scan for 48+ hours (§4.4). The goal is to get it moving again or, failing recovery, convert to a Lost-in-Transit / FreightSafe outcome. A delay ticket is only resolved and closed once the item is delivered.
Both success paths (back-on-track and retrieved) only close once delivered. Maps to spec §4.4 (no-movement) + §4.6 (investigation) + outcome tags §2.1.

Dispute of Delivery

Customer disputes a delivery scan. Evidence is requested; if it confirms correct delivery the case resolves. If not (e.g. delivered to an incorrect address), an investigation and retrieval are initiated, and the case closes as retrieved & redelivered, located, or Lost in Transit.
Closure outcomes: Resolved (retrieved & redelivered), Located / found, or Lost in Transit. Maps to live reasons: Dispute of Delivery, POD Request, ATL Image Request, Redelivery Request.

Damaged Item

First check whether the customer notified within the eligible-claim timeframe (a claim checkbox). Then investigate the damage to determine where the fault lies — MailPlus, the customer, or StarTrack. Carrier/MailPlus fault leads to a claim; customer error is advised and closed.
Claim eligibility is set by the "notified within timeframe" check. MailPlus / StarTrack fault → claim information sent & ticket closed; customer error → advise & close.

Check Address — depot-created tickets

Raised by a depot when a delivery address needs checking. A simple, linear flow: the ticket is opened, the customer is contacted to confirm the address, the information is provided, and the ticket is closed.
Depot-created ticket type. Sits alongside the Operations ticket stream; no investigation or claim path.

Multi-consignment master case

A depot-wide incident: many barcodes raised at once via bulk CSV upload (validated row-by-row), grouped into one master case with a single customer conversation. Each barcode resolves independently, and the case only closes once all are resolved (§6).
Maps to spec §6 (master case) + §3.1 (bulk upload with row validation). Individual outcomes tracked per barcode.
